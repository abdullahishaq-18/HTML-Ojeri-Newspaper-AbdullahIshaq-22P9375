Daily Jang (English Edition)

Rawalpindi — April 11, 1988

Deadly Explosions at Military Storage Camp Shake Rawalpindi

Over 100 Blasts Rock City, Nearly 1,000 Injured

Thick smoke rises from the Ojhri ammunition depot as a series of explosions rock the area.

City Rocked by Chain Explosions

A massive series of explosions erupted at the Ojhri Camp ammunition storage facility early Monday morning, sending shockwaves across Rawalpindi and Islamabad. Residents reported continuous blasts lasting several hours, causing widespread panic and confusion.

Live rockets and ammunition were hurled into the air, landing unpredictably across populated areas. Roads were blocked, electricity was disrupted, and thick smoke could be seen from miles away.

Emergency services struggled to reach affected zones as fires and debris made rescue operations extremely difficult.

Casualties Overwhelm Hospitals

Hospitals in Rawalpindi and Islamabad were flooded with injured civilians and military personnel. Doctors declared emergency conditions as medical staff worked tirelessly to provide treatment amid limited resources.

Several soldiers and civilians sustained severe injuries caused by flying debris and falling ammunition. Ambulances made repeated trips to the explosion site as rescue efforts continued throughout the day.

Injured soldiers are rushed on stretchers to ambulances following the deadly explosions.

Political and Military Response

The federal government ordered an immediate inquiry into the incident, calling it a serious lapse in safety and management. Senior officials assured the public that those responsible would be held accountable.

Among the injured was then Federal Minister for Petroleum, Khaqan Abbasi, who sustained wounds after being caught in the fallout of the explosions. His condition was reported to be stable.

Opposition leaders criticized the handling of sensitive military assets and demanded transparency in the investigation process.
